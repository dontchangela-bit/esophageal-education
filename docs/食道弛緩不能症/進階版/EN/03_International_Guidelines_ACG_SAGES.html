Esophageal Achalasia — International Guidelines Summary

Overview

This article consolidates the most relevant international clinical guidelines for the diagnosis and management of esophageal achalasia, including:

  1. ACG Clinical Guidelines (American College of Gastroenterology)
  2. ASGE 2020 Guideline (American Society for Gastrointestinal Endoscopy)
  3. SAGES 2024 POEM Update (Society of American Gastrointestinal and Endoscopic Surgeons)
  4. EAES 2026 Multi-Society Guideline (European Association for Endoscopic Surgery, in development)

All guidelines employ the GRADE methodology (Grading of Recommendations, Assessment, Development and Evaluations) for evidence assessment and recommendation grading.

Introduction to the GRADE Methodology

Evidence Quality Definition
High Further research is very unlikely to change confidence in the effect estimate
Moderate Further research is likely to have an important impact on confidence in the effect estimate and may change the estimate
Low Further research is very likely to have an important impact on confidence in the effect estimate and is likely to change the estimate
Very Low The effect estimate is very uncertain
Recommendation Strength Definition
Strong Applicable to most patients; clinicians should follow
Conditional Depends on individual patient circumstances; requires consideration of patient preferences and clinical judgment

I. ACG Clinical Guidelines

Guideline Background

  • Issuing body: American College of Gastroenterology (ACG)
  • Employs GRADE methodology
  • Covers comprehensive diagnostic and treatment recommendations for achalasia
  • First official adoption of the Chicago Classification three-subtype system

Diagnostic Recommendations

Recommendation Evidence Quality Strength
HRM is recommended as the preferred diagnostic tool for achalasia Low Conditional
All patients with suspected achalasia should undergo upper endoscopy to rule out pseudoachalasia Very Low Strong
Barium esophagram can serve as an adjunctive diagnostic tool to assess esophageal morphology and emptying Very Low Conditional
Achalasia should be classified into Types I, II, and III based on HRM findings Low Strong
Timed Barium Esophagram (TBE) can be used for post-treatment follow-up Very Low Conditional

Treatment Recommendations

Recommendation Evidence Quality Strength
Eligible achalasia patients should receive definitive treatment (PD, LHM, or POEM) rather than medication or BTX alone Moderate Strong
PD and LHM are both effective first-line treatments Moderate Strong
PD should follow a graded dilation protocol, starting with a 30 mm balloon Low Conditional
LHM should be combined with partial fundoplication to reduce post-operative GERD Moderate Strong
BTX injection should be reserved for patients unsuitable for PD or surgery Moderate Strong
Medical therapy (calcium channel blockers, nitrates) should only serve as a bridge to other treatments Low Conditional
Post-treatment follow-up should be conducted regularly to assess symptom improvement and complications Very Low Conditional

ACG on Subtype-Specific Treatment

Subtype ACG Treatment Recommendation
Type I PD or LHM are both viable; evidence does not clearly favor either
Type II PD or LHM are both viable; this subtype responds best to all treatments
Type III LHM or POEM recommended; PD efficacy is inferior

II. ASGE 2020 Guideline

Guideline Background

  • Issuing body: American Society for Gastrointestinal Endoscopy (ASGE)
  • Published: 2020
  • Focuses on the role of endoscopy in the diagnosis and treatment of achalasia

Key Recommendation Summary

Recommendation Evidence Quality Strength
POEM is an effective treatment option for achalasia with short-term efficacy comparable to LHM Moderate Conditional
Post-POEM GERD should be monitored; routine pH monitoring and endoscopic follow-up should be considered Low Conditional
Graded pneumatic dilation remains an effective first-line treatment for achalasia Moderate Strong
The role of endoscopy in achalasia includes diagnosis (ruling out pseudoachalasia) and treatment (BTX, PD, POEM) — Expert consensus
BTX injection is appropriate for high surgical risk patients or as temporary treatment Moderate Conditional

ASGE Technical Recommendations for POEM

Technical Aspect ASGE Recommendation
Tunnel entry site Approximately 10–12 cm proximal to the EGJ
Myotomy orientation Either anterior or posterior is acceptable
Myotomy extent At least 6 cm on the esophageal side; extend 2–3 cm beyond the EGJ on the gastric side
Special consideration for Type III Esophageal myotomy can be extended to > 10 cm to cover the spastic segment
Tunnel closure Endoscopic clips to close the mucosal entry

III. SAGES 2024 POEM Update

Guideline Background

  • Issuing body: Society of American Gastrointestinal and Endoscopic Surgeons (SAGES)
  • Published: 2024
  • First standalone SAGES guideline update dedicated to POEM
  • Incorporates the latest randomized controlled trials (RCTs) and systematic reviews

Core Recommendations

Recommendation Evidence Quality Strength
POEM can serve as a first-line definitive treatment for achalasia Moderate Conditional
POEM is recommended over PD (pneumatic dilation) Low to Moderate Conditional
POEM is recommended as first-line for Type III achalasia Low Conditional
POEM and LHM have comparable short- to medium-term efficacy; they can serve as alternatives Moderate Conditional
Post-POEM GERD should be routinely assessed; PPI prophylaxis or treatment should be considered Low to Moderate Conditional
POEM should be performed at centers with adequate experience Expert consensus Strong
Operators should complete adequate training (recommended ≥ 20 supervised cases) Expert consensus Strong

Key Updates from SAGES 2024

1. Rationale for POEM over PD: - Multiple RCTs show POEM has superior 2-year symptom relief rates compared to PD - POEM has a lower retreatment rate than PD - POEM provides more durable sphincter decompression

2. Positioning of POEM vs LHM: - Comparable short- to medium-term efficacy (2–5 year data) - LHM + Fundoplication has an advantage in GERD control - Selection should be based on: subtype, GERD risk, operator experience, patient preference - SAGES does not explicitly recommend POEM over LHM (both listed as first-line options)

3. GERD Management Recommendations: - All post-POEM patients should be informed of GERD risk - Objective GERD assessment (pH monitoring +/- endoscopy) is recommended within 3–12 months post-operatively - PPI should be prescribed for symptomatic patients or those with confirmed abnormal acid exposure - Long-term monitoring of esophageal mucosal changes

IV. EAES 2026 Multi-Society Guideline (In Development)

EAES 2026 Multi-Society Clinical Practice Guideline (In Development – Expected Publication 2026)

Item Details
Lead society EAES (European Association for Endoscopic Surgery)
Collaborating societies AATS (American Association for Thoracic Surgery), SAGES (Society of American Gastrointestinal and Endoscopic Surgeons)
Methodology Systematic literature search through July 2025 + GRADE methodology
Expert panel International multidisciplinary team: 4 GI surgeons, 2 gastroenterologists, 1 thoracic surgeon, 2 patient representatives
Target journal Surgical Endoscopy (official journal of EAES and SAGES)
Expected impact Will provide transatlantic unified recommendations integrating the latest evidence from Europe and America

This is the first achalasia treatment guideline jointly developed by three major European and American societies, and is expected to have a significant impact on global treatment consensus.

Clinical Practice Recommendations

Integrated Practical Recommendations from All Guidelines

  1. Diagnostic workflow: Follow the ACG-recommended standardized diagnostic pathway (EGD → HRM → subtyping → TBE)
  2. Treatment selection:
    • Type I / II: POEM, LHM, and PD are all reasonable options; decide based on patient factors and physician expertise
    • Type III: Prioritize POEM (SAGES 2024)
    • Patients unfit for surgery: BTX injection
  3. POEM standards: Should be performed at experienced centers (SAGES 2024 strong recommendation)
  4. Post-treatment follow-up: Routine GERD monitoring after POEM; Eckardt Score follow-up after all treatments
  5. Retreatment: Multiple options available after initial failure; assess on a case-by-case basis

Key Points Summary

Key Point Explanation
