# Leading Hospitals and Outcomes

## Introduction

Esophagectomy is one of the surgical procedures in which the volume-outcome relationship is most pronounced. International studies consistently demonstrate that high-volume centers (≥25 cases/year) have significantly lower postoperative mortality and complication rates than low-volume centers. This chapter compiles treatment outcomes from representative minimally invasive esophageal cancer surgery centers in the United States and Taiwan.

## Volume-Outcome Data

### International Data Summary

| Annual Volume Tier | 30-Day Mortality | 90-Day Mortality | Major Complication Rate | Failure to Rescue (FTR) |
|-------------------|-----------------|-----------------|------------------------|-------------------------|
| < 5 cases/year (very low) | 16-23% | 20-30% | High | > 30% |
| 5-24 cases/year (low-moderate) | 8-12% | 10-15% | Moderate | 15-25% |
| 25-59 cases/year (high) | 5-8% | 6-10% | Moderate-low | 10-15% |
| ≥ 60 cases/year (very high) | **3-5%** | **4-6%** | **Low** | **< 10%** |

### Advantages of High-Volume Centers

| Metric | High-Volume Center | Low-Volume Center | Magnitude of Difference |
|--------|--------------------|--------------------|------------------------|
| 30-day postoperative mortality | 3-5% | 16-23% | 3-5 fold |
| ICU length of stay | 1-3 days | 3-7 days | ~2 fold |
| Hospital length of stay | 8-12 days | 14-21 days | ~1.5-2 fold |
| Unplanned reoperation rate | < 5% | 8-15% | ~2-3 fold |
| 30-day readmission rate | 5-10% | 15-25% | ~2 fold |
| Long-term survival | Better | Worse | Statistically significant |

## Hospital Selection and Referral Recommendations

### International Guidelines on Surgical Volume

| Guideline | Recommended Minimum Volume | Optimal Outcomes Volume |
|-----------|---------------------------|------------------------|
| NCCN 2025 | Referral to high-volume center recommended | No specific number defined |
| ESMO 2022 | ≥ 20 cases/year | ≥ 50 cases/year |
| UK NHS | ≥ 40 cases/year (center level) | - |
| Netherlands DUCA | ≥ 20 cases/year | - |
| Literature consensus | ≥ 25 cases/year | **≥ 60 cases/year** |

### Referral Decision Considerations

Referral to a high-volume center is recommended in the following situations:

1. Current hospital annual esophagectomy volume < 20 cases
2. Patient desires minimally invasive surgery but current hospital lacks MIE/RAMIE experience
3. Complex cases (post-neoadjuvant therapy, reoperation, severe comorbidities)
4. Elderly patients or those with poor performance status (requiring more refined risk assessment)
5. Tumors in special locations (cervical esophagus, GEJ)

### Indicators for Evaluating Hospital Quality

- STS quality certification or NCI-designated cancer center status
- Comprehensive MDT team (surgery, medical oncology, radiation oncology, nutrition, rehabilitation)
- Participation in prospective clinical trials
- Public reporting of surgical volumes and outcomes
- ICU care quality and staffing
- Complication management capability and experience

## Special Considerations for the Taiwan Healthcare System

### Hospital Selection Under National Health Insurance

- Under Taiwan's National Health Insurance system, patients are free to choose their hospital
- Da Vinci robotic surgery may require out-of-pocket payment for some components
- Most medical centers offer comprehensive esophageal cancer MDT care
- Prioritize medical centers with higher annual surgical volumes
