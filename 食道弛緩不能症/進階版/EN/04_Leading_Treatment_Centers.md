# Esophageal Achalasia — Leading Treatment Centers

## Introduction

Treatment outcomes for esophageal achalasia are closely related to the experience of the medical team, particularly for procedures such as POEM (Peroral Endoscopic Myotomy) and Heller myotomy. **High-volume centers** generally demonstrate significantly better treatment success rates and safety profiles compared to low-volume centers.

The SAGES 2024 guidelines **strongly recommend** that achalasia treatment be performed at centers with adequate experience.

This article lists representative treatment centers in the United States and Taiwan for clinical reference.

## Factors to Consider When Choosing a Treatment Center

### Recommendations for Patients

When selecting a treatment center, consider the following factors:

| Factor | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Procedure volume** | Higher-volume centers typically have better success rates and safety profiles. Choose a center performing ≥ 10–20 POEM cases per year |
| **Operator experience** | Inquire about the performing physician's personal experience and cumulative case count |
| **Complete team** | An ideal center should have gastroenterology, digestive surgery, and an esophageal function testing laboratory |
| **Follow-up system** | Does the center have a comprehensive post-operative follow-up program (including pH monitoring, endoscopic follow-up, etc.)? |
| **Complication management capability** | Can the center manage procedure-related complications (e.g., perforation)? |
| **Geographic convenience** | Long-term follow-up requires regular clinic visits; distance is a practical consideration |
| **Second-line treatment capability** | If initial treatment fails, does the center offer multiple retreatment options? |

### Recommendations for Healthcare Professionals

| Referral Indicator | Recommendation |
|--------------------|----------------|
| Diagnostically challenging cases | Refer to centers with comprehensive esophageal function testing |
| Type III achalasia | Refer to centers with POEM experience |
| Prior treatment failure | Refer to high-volume centers for second-line option assessment |
| Sigmoid esophagus / megaesophagus | Refer to centers experienced in managing complex esophageal disease |
| Young patients | Refer to centers offering multiple treatment options with long-term follow-up systems |

## Quality Metrics Reference

The following are reference quality indicators for evaluating achalasia treatment:

| Quality Indicator | Target |
|-------------------|--------|
| Post-POEM Eckardt Score ≤ 3 rate | ≥ 85% |
| POEM-related serious complication rate | < 3% |
| LHM esophageal perforation rate | < 5% |
| PD perforation rate | < 3% |
| 30-day post-procedure readmission rate | < 5% |
| Post-POEM GERD monitoring completion rate | ≥ 80% |
| 1-year post-treatment follow-up completion rate | ≥ 80% |
