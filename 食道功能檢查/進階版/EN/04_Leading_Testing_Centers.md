# Leading Esophageal Function Testing Centers

## Introduction

The quality of esophageal function testing is highly dependent on equipment, the experience of the technical team, and interpretation expertise. This section compiles information on major international and domestic esophageal function testing centers for reference by clinicians and patients.

### US Center Highlights

- **Northwestern Medicine**: As the birthplace of the Chicago Classification, it holds a global leadership position in esophageal manometry research and standardization of clinical interpretation. The research team continues to advance the development of HRM and FLIP technology.

- **USC Keck**: Offers the most comprehensive known combination of esophageal function tests, including HRM with impedance, 24-hour pH-impedance monitoring, extended 96-hour Bravo wireless pH monitoring, and FLIP.

- **UCSF**: Established its dedicated esophageal motility center in 1999, making it one of the earliest institutions in the US to specialize in this field, with extensive long-term clinical experience.

- **UC San Diego**: Has made significant contributions to the clinical application and research of FLIP technology, and is an important driver of its development.

| Chang Gung Memorial Hospital | Division of Gastroenterology and Hepatology | HRM, pH monitoring | Comprehensive hospital system with locations across northern, central, and southern Taiwan | Cross-campus services |

### Current Status in Taiwan

#### Testing Capacity

- Institutions with HRM equipment in Taiwan are primarily medical centers
- Some regional hospitals may not yet have high-resolution systems and may still use conventional manometry
- pH-impedance monitoring equipment is less widely available than HRM
- FLIP and 96-hour Bravo monitoring have limited availability in Taiwan

#### Referral Recommendations

- Patients requiring esophageal function testing should be referred to medical centers with comprehensive testing equipment if their local hospital is not equipped
- Tri-Service General Hospital offers advantages in workflow efficiency and specialization due to its dedicated esophageal function testing center
- Taipei Veterans General Hospital has the complete combination of HRM and 24-hour pH-impedance monitoring, suitable for patients requiring comprehensive evaluation

#### Interpretation Standards

- Major centers in Taiwan follow Chicago Classification v4.0 for HRM interpretation
- Reflux monitoring is interpreted according to Lyon Consensus 2.0
- It is recommended to have results interpreted by physicians with extensive experience in esophageal function testing to ensure accuracy

## Factors to Consider When Choosing a Testing Center

### Recommendations for Clinicians

| Factor | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Equipment completeness | Availability of HRM, pH-impedance monitoring, FLIP, and other comprehensive testing tools |
| Testing volume | High-volume centers typically have richer experience and more consistent quality |
| Interpretation expertise | Whether the interpreting physician is well-versed in CCv4.0 and Lyon 2.0 |
| Multidisciplinary team | Availability of gastroenterology, surgery, speech therapy, and other multidisciplinary collaboration |
| Research participation | Centers involved in international research typically offer the latest diagnostic and treatment approaches |
| Accessibility | Transportation convenience and wait times for patients |

### Recommendations for Patients

- Choose a **medical center** or a hospital with expertise in esophageal function testing
- Confirm that the facility uses a **high-resolution manometry (HRM)** system rather than a conventional system
- Verify that the available tests match your clinical needs
- Ask about report turnaround time and follow-up arrangements
- If multiple tests are needed, prioritize centers where all tests can be completed at a single institution

## International Trends

### Future Directions in Esophageal Function Testing

1. **Artificial intelligence (AI)-assisted interpretation**: Multiple centers are developing AI systems to assist in automatic interpretation of HRM and reflux monitoring
2. **Expanding role of FLIP**: An increasing number of centers are incorporating FLIP into routine esophageal function evaluation
3. **Remote interpretation**: Digital platforms are making remote expert interpretation possible
4. **Standardized training**: International organizations (such as ISDE, AGA) are promoting standardized training programs for esophageal function testing
5. **Integrated assessment**: Comprehensive diagnostic models combining HRM, reflux monitoring, FLIP, barium esophagram, and other tests
