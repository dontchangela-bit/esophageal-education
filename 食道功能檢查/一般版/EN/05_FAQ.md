# Esophageal Function Testing -- Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

## Introduction

The following is a compilation of the most common questions patients ask before and after esophageal function testing. We hope this helps you feel more at ease about your upcoming test.

---

### Q1: Does esophageal function testing hurt?

**Answer**: Most patients describe the experience as "uncomfortable but not painful."

- Before placing the nasal catheter, the healthcare team will spray a local anesthetic into the nasal passage, greatly reducing discomfort
- You may experience a mild gag reflex when the catheter passes through the throat; this is a normal body response that usually passes within seconds
- After the catheter is in position, most people gradually adjust and discomfort decreases significantly
- On a pain scale of 0-10, most patients rate the discomfort at approximately 2-4
- FLIP is performed under sedation and typically causes no discomfort

---

### Q2: How long does the entire test take?

**Answer**: Depending on the type of test, the duration varies considerably.

| Test Type | Time at Hospital | Total Test Duration |
|-----------|-----------------|-------------------|
| HRM (Manometry) | About 30-45 minutes | Test itself about 10-15 minutes |
| 24h pH Monitoring | Placement about 30 min + removal next day about 15 min | 24 hours wearing the catheter |
| pH-Impedance Monitoring | Placement about 30 min + removal next day about 15 min | 24 hours wearing the catheter |
| Bravo Wireless Monitoring | About 1-2 hours (including endoscopy) | 48-96 hours wearing the receiver |
| FLIP | About 1-2 hours (including endoscopy) | Test itself about 5-10 minutes |

---

### Q3: Can I eat right after the test?

**Answer**: Yes.

- **HRM**: You may eat immediately after the catheter is removed. We recommend starting with some warm water, then gradually resuming normal diet
- **24h pH / Impedance Monitoring**: You may eat normally after the catheter is placed (normal eating during monitoring is needed to record reflux). You may also eat normally after the catheter is removed
- **Bravo / FLIP**: If sedation was used, wait until fully awake before eating (usually about 1-2 hours). Start with clear liquids, then gradually resume solid food

---

### Q4: Do I need someone to drive me?

**Answer**: It depends on the type of test.

- **HRM and 24h pH Monitoring**: Usually **not needed**. These tests do not use sedation, and you may drive yourself or use public transportation
- **Bravo Wireless Monitoring and FLIP**: **Yes**, you need someone to drive you. These tests are typically performed under endoscopic sedation, and you must not operate any vehicle for 24 hours after sedation
- If you are particularly anxious or nervous, we recommend having someone accompany you to any test

---

### Q5: Will the nasal catheter be very uncomfortable? Can I tolerate it for 24 hours?

**Answer**: The discomfort from the nasal catheter varies among individuals, but most people complete the test successfully.

- The catheter is very thin (approximately 4 mm, much thinner than a standard nasogastric tube)
- Discomfort is most noticeable at the time of initial placement, but most people gradually adapt within 30 minutes to 1 hour
- During 24-hour monitoring, you can speak, work, and move about normally
- When sleeping, simply place the recorder next to your pillow; most people can sleep normally
- If you truly cannot tolerate the nasal catheter, discuss switching to Bravo wireless monitoring with your physician

---

### Q6: Can I talk during the test?

**Answer**: Yes, but try to minimize talking.

- After the catheter is in place, you can speak
- During formal HRM testing, the healthcare team will ask you to **remain quiet** between swallow tests, as speaking or swallowing saliva can affect measurement results
- If you feel any discomfort or need to pause, you may use hand gestures or brief words to notify the healthcare team
- During 24-hour monitoring, you may speak normally without affecting the recording

---

### Q7: What if I can't swallow the tube?

**Answer**: This is a common concern, but in practice, the vast majority of people complete the procedure successfully.

- The healthcare team is professionally trained and has extensive experience
- Local anesthesia greatly reduces sensitivity
- By coordinating your swallowing with the catheter insertion, the catheter usually passes through the throat smoothly
- If one nostril proves difficult, the other side can be tried
- In very rare cases where insertion is truly not possible, the physician will consider alternative options
- **Relaxation is key**: Deep breathing, relaxing your shoulders, and trusting the healthcare team's guidance

---

### Q8: How long until I receive the test report?

**Answer**: Typically 3 to 10 business days.

- HRM and FLIP reports are usually faster, about 3-7 business days
- pH monitoring reports may take 5-10 business days due to the larger volume of data
- Once the report is ready, your physician will explain the results in detail at your follow-up visit
- Some hospitals may offer online access to view reports
- If you need results sooner (for example, for surgical scheduling), discuss this with the healthcare team

---

### Q9: Will I need to repeat the esophageal function test?

**Answer**: Not necessarily; it depends on the clinical situation.

- **Most patients only need one test** to obtain sufficient diagnostic information
- Situations where a repeat test may be needed:
  - Initial test results are unclear or borderline
  - Post-treatment effectiveness evaluation
  - Before and after surgery comparison
  - Significant change in symptoms requiring re-evaluation
- The procedure and preparation for a repeat test are the same as the first time

---

### Q10: Can children undergo esophageal function testing?

**Answer**: Yes, but there are some special considerations.

- Esophageal function testing in children must be performed by a pediatric gastroenterology specialist
- Thinner catheters are used, appropriate for a child's esophageal size
- Younger children may require sedation
- Older, cooperative children can complete the test while awake
- Pre-test preparation and fasting requirements are similar to adults but adjusted for age
- Please discuss the details with a pediatric gastroenterologist

---

### Q11: I'm taking anticoagulants (blood thinners). Can I still have the test?

**Answer**: In most cases, yes, but please inform your physician in advance.

- Esophageal manometry (HRM) and pH monitoring are generally safe and do not require discontinuation of anticoagulants
- Aspirin usually does not need to be stopped
- Warfarin or novel oral anticoagulants (NOACs): In most cases, discontinuation is not needed, but please confirm with your physician
- For Bravo capsule placement, since the capsule must adhere to the esophageal wall, the physician will specifically assess bleeding risk
- **Key point**: Always inform your physician of all medications you are taking before the test

---

### Q12: Is the test covered by insurance? Will there be out-of-pocket costs?

**Answer**: Some tests are covered by National Health Insurance (NHI), while others may require out-of-pocket payment.

- **HRM (Manometry)**: Generally covered by NHI for eligible indications
- **24h pH Monitoring**: Generally covered by NHI for eligible indications
- **pH-Impedance Monitoring**: Some hospitals may require self-pay or partial self-pay
- **Bravo Wireless Monitoring**: The capsule is a consumable and may require self-pay (higher cost)
- **FLIP**: Currently requires self-pay at most hospitals
- Actual costs vary by hospital and individual circumstances. We recommend inquiring with your healthcare team in advance

> **Note**: Insurance coverage policies may change at any time. Please refer to the latest announcements from your hospital and the guidance of your healthcare team.

---

### Q13: Can I shower normally during 24-hour monitoring?

**Answer**: **Showering or bathing is not recommended** while wearing the catheter and recorder.

- The recorder is not waterproof and may malfunction if exposed to moisture
- The tape securing the catheter may come loose when wet
- We recommend showering before the catheter is placed
- You may use a damp towel to wipe your body (avoiding the catheter and recorder area)
- You may shower normally once the catheter is removed

---

### Q14: I'm very nervous before the test. Can I take a sedative?

**Answer**: Generally, taking a sedative before HRM or pH monitoring is not recommended.

- Sedatives may affect esophageal muscle function, thereby affecting the accuracy of the test results
- If you are very anxious, please inform your physician in advance; the physician will assess the appropriate course of action
- Some hospitals offer pre-test educational videos or simulation experiences to help reduce anxiety
- Deep breathing, relaxation techniques, and positive self-talk are usually very helpful
- Bravo and FLIP are performed under endoscopic sedation and do not require additional sedation

---

### Q15: The test results are normal, but I still have symptoms. What should I do?

**Answer**: This is a common clinical scenario and does not mean your symptoms are not real.

- Normal esophageal function test results mean that esophageal motor function and reflux levels are within the normal range
- However, symptoms may arise from other causes, such as:
  - Functional esophageal disorder: increased esophageal sensitivity to normal stimuli
  - Esophageal hypersensitivity
  - Psychologically related symptoms (stress, anxiety)
- Your physician will arrange further investigations or treatment based on the overall clinical assessment
- Do not dismiss your symptoms just because the test results are normal. Continued communication with your physician is important
