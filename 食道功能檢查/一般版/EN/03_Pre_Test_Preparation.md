# Pre-Test Preparation for Esophageal Function Testing

## Introduction

Adequate pre-test preparation is essential for ensuring a smooth procedure and accurate, reliable results. This section provides detailed instructions on how to prepare for each type of esophageal function test. Please read carefully and follow the instructions provided by your healthcare team.

---

## 1. Fasting Requirements

### Common Requirements for All Esophageal Function Tests

- **Fast for at least 6 hours before the test** (including solid food and liquids)
- Small amounts of clear water may be consumed up to 2 hours before the test; after that, nothing by mouth
- Purpose of fasting:
  - To prevent vomiting during the procedure, which could lead to aspiration pneumonia
  - To ensure the esophagus is empty so test results are not affected
  - To reduce discomfort during the test

### Fasting Quick Reference

| Item | Last Allowed Intake Time |
|------|------------------------|
| Solid food (meals, snacks) | **6 hours or more** before the test |
| Milk, beverages with pulp | **6 hours or more** before the test |
| Clear liquids (water, pulp-free juice) | **2 hours or more** before the test |
| Chewing gum, candy | **6 hours or more** before the test |

> **Practical tip**: If your test is scheduled in the morning, the simplest approach is to **have nothing to eat or drink after midnight the night before**.

---

## 2. Medication Adjustments (Very Important)

Certain medications can affect the results of esophageal function tests, particularly during **reflux monitoring**. Please confirm with your physician whether you need to stop any medications.

### Medications That Need to Be Stopped

#### Proton Pump Inhibitors (PPI)

Discontinuation period: **7 days before the test**

Common medications:
- Nexium (esomeprazole)
- Pariet (rabeprazole)
- Takepron (lansoprazole)
- Omeprazole
- Pantoprazole

#### H2 Receptor Antagonists

Discontinuation period: **3 days before the test**

Common medications:
- Famotidine
- Ranitidine (withdrawn in some regions)
- Cimetidine

#### Antacids

Discontinuation period: **6 to 12 hours before the test**

Common medications:
- Antacid tablets / suspension
- Aluminum-magnesium combinations
- Magnesium oxide (MgO)
- Calcium carbonate chewable tablets

#### Prokinetics

Discontinuation period: **3 days before the test** (follow physician instructions)

Common medications:
- Mosapride
- Domperidone

### Medication Discontinuation Summary

| Medication Class | Discontinuation Period | Common Examples |
|-----------------|----------------------|----------------|
| PPI (Proton Pump Inhibitors) | 7 days before the test | Nexium, Pariet, Takepron |
| H2 Receptor Antagonists | 3 days before the test | Famotidine |
| Antacids | 6-12 hours before the test | Antacid tablets, antacid powder |
| Prokinetics | 3 days before the test | Mosapride |

### Important Notes

> **Please note**: The above medication discontinuation recommendations primarily apply to **reflux monitoring tests** (pH monitoring, pH-impedance monitoring, Bravo monitoring). If you are only undergoing **esophageal manometry (HRM)**, the discontinuation requirements may differ. Please follow your physician's instructions.

> **Do not stop medications on your own**: Always confirm with your physician before stopping any medication. Certain medications (such as blood pressure medications, cardiac medications, and antiepileptic drugs) should not be stopped without medical guidance.

## 3. Preparation for Special Circumstances

### Patients with Diabetes

- **Skip** oral hypoglycemic agents on the morning of the test
- Patients using insulin should discuss dose adjustments with their physician in advance
- Check your blood sugar before the test; notify the healthcare team if your blood sugar is too low
- Schedule the test for the **first slot in the morning** if possible to minimize fasting time
- Bring candy or a sugary drink for use after the test

### Patients Taking Anticoagulants or Antiplatelet Agents

- Aspirin: Generally **does not need to be stopped**
- Warfarin, novel oral anticoagulants (NOACs): Discuss with your physician whether adjustments are needed
- Clopidogrel: Discuss with your physician

### Pregnant Women or Women Who May Be Pregnant

- Please inform the healthcare team before the test
- Esophageal function tests do not involve radiation and are generally safe
- However, the physician needs to evaluate the necessity and timing of the test

### Nasal or Throat Issues

- Patients with severe nasal congestion, nasal polyps, or deviated septum should inform the team in advance
- Those who have had prior nasal or throat surgery should inform the team in advance
- The physician may need to adjust the catheter placement technique

## 4. What to Bring on the Day of the Test

### Required Items Checklist

- [ ] Health insurance card
- [ ] Identification card or other ID
- [ ] Current **medication list** or pharmacy bags
- [ ] Test order or referral form from your physician
- [ ] Previous related test reports (if available)

### Recommended Items

- [ ] Comfortable, loose-fitting clothing (especially for 24-hour monitoring with a recorder)
- [ ] Top with front buttons or a zipper (easy to put on/remove and attach the recorder)
- [ ] Small amount of cash or credit card (some tests may have out-of-pocket costs)
- [ ] Mobile phone and charger (you may use your phone normally during 24-hour monitoring)
- [ ] Notepad or phone memo app (for recording symptoms and meal times)
- [ ] Light snack (for eating after the test is complete)

### Clothing Recommendations

- **Recommended**: Comfortable cotton top, preferably a button-down or zip-up style
- **Avoid**: Tight-fitting clothing, turtleneck tops, one-piece dresses
- **Additional recommendation for 24-hour monitoring**: Belt or pants with pockets (for carrying the recorder)

---

## 5. Transportation Arrangements

### Situations Where an Escort Is Needed

| Test Type | Escort Needed for Transportation? |
|-----------|----------------------------------|
| HRM (Manometry) | Usually not needed; you may leave on your own |
| 24h pH / pH-Impedance Monitoring | Usually not needed (one visit for placement, one for removal) |
| Bravo Wireless Monitoring | **Yes** (sedation may be used) |
| FLIP | **Yes** (usually performed under endoscopic sedation) |

> **Important**: If sedatives or anesthesia are used during the test, **do not drive yourself**. Please arrange for a family member or friend to provide transportation, or use public transit / taxi.

## 6. Mental Preparation

### Common Concerns and Suggestions

**"I'm afraid of having a tube inserted through my nose"**
- The healthcare team will spray a local anesthetic into the nasal passage, greatly reducing discomfort
- The catheter is very thin (approximately 4 mm) -- much thinner than an endoscope
- The placement process usually takes only 1-2 minutes
- Following the healthcare team's instructions to swallow on cue will make the process smoother

**"I'm worried I'll keep gagging"**
- You may experience a mild gag reflex during initial placement; this is a normal body response that typically passes within seconds
- After the catheter is in position, the gagging sensation usually subsides gradually
- Deep breathing and relaxation help reduce discomfort
- If discomfort persists, inform the healthcare team

**"Will it be very inconvenient to live with a nasal catheter for 24 hours?"**
- Most daily activities can be carried out normally
- You can work normally (light office work)
- You can speak and eat normally
- Avoid vigorous exercise and showering (to protect the recorder)

## Complete Pre-Test Preparation Checklist

The following is a printable preparation checklist:

### 7 Days Before the Test
- [ ] Stop PPI as directed by your physician (if applicable)
- [ ] Confirm the date and time for returning to have the recorder removed (if applicable)

### 3 Days Before the Test
- [ ] Stop H2 receptor antagonists as directed (if applicable)
- [ ] Stop prokinetics as directed (if applicable)

### 1 Day Before the Test
- [ ] Confirm the test time and location
- [ ] Arrange transportation (if sedation will be used)
- [ ] Prepare all items to bring
- [ ] Select appropriate clothing

### 6-12 Hours Before the Test
- [ ] Stop antacids as directed
- [ ] Begin fasting (solid food and milk, etc.)

### Day of the Test
- [ ] Nothing by mouth starting 2 hours before the test
- [ ] Bring all necessary items
- [ ] Wear comfortable, loose-fitting clothing
- [ ] Diabetes patients: Check blood sugar, adjust medications as directed
- [ ] Arrive at the test location on time
